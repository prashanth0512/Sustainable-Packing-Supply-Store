The True Cost of Plastic Packaging: A Business Case for Going Compostable

MO
Marcus Okafor Head of Sustainability · Jan 14, 2026

Every year, businesses spend billions on plastic packaging — and then spend again to dispose of it, manage customer complaints about it, and navigate ever-tightening regulations around it. The true cost of plastic is almost always underestimated.

The Hidden Costs of Plastic

When procurement teams calculate packaging costs, they typically look at the unit price. But there are at least four additional cost categories that rarely appear on a single spreadsheet:

  • Disposal fees: In many jurisdictions, businesses now pay extended producer responsibility (EPR) fees based on the weight and type of plastic they put into the market.
  • Customer churn: A 2025 Deloitte study found 38% of consumers have stopped purchasing from a brand due to excessive or non-recyclable packaging.
  • Regulatory risk: The EU Single-Use Plastics Directive and US state-level EPR laws are expanding rapidly. Compliance retrofitting is expensive.
  • Brand perception: In B2B, procurement teams at major retailers are now requiring sustainability documentation from suppliers.

The Compostable Business Case

Switching to compostable packaging isn't an environmental charity — it's increasingly a financial decision. Here's what the numbers look like for an e-commerce business shipping 10,000 orders per month:

💡 Real numbers from a VerdantPack customer:

A mid-size skincare brand switched from LDPE poly mailers to compostable mailers. Their per-unit cost increased by $0.06 — but they saw a 22% increase in 5-star reviews mentioning packaging, a 15% reduction in returns (better cushioning), and were able to highlight the switch in their Series B pitch deck as a key ESG metric.

How to Make the Transition Practical

The biggest barrier to switching isn't price — it's operational complexity. Changing packaging specs means updating warehouse systems, retraining staff, and often renegotiating with fulfillment partners. Here's how to make it frictionless:

  1. Start with your highest-volume SKU and run a 30-day pilot.
  2. Negotiate a dual-stock period where both old and new packaging are available.
  3. Use your dashboard to track cost-per-shipment before and after the switch.
  4. Communicate the change proactively to customers — they notice and they appreciate it.

The Bottom Line

The plastic-to-compostable transition is no longer a question of if, but when and how efficiently. The businesses that move first gain the brand narrative, the regulatory head start, and increasingly, the better unit economics.
